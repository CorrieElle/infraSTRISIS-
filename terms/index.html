4. The Diagnostic Process

Phase 0: Pre-Work Submission

After booking your Diagnostic Session via Calendly, you will receive pre-work instructions. You must submit the following to corrie@infrastrisis.com:

Required:

  • Crisis Narrative (3 pages maximum): What's breaking, what triggered it, what success looks like
  • Organizational Structure (1 document): Who reports to whom (org chart, list, or narrative)
  • Attempted Solutions (1 page): What you've already tried and what happened

Optional:

  • 2-minute video walkthrough
  • Up to 3 supporting documents (recent communications, existing SOPs, budget constraints)

Hard Limits: Maximum 3 pages per document • Maximum 3 documents total for reference materials • No "library dumps" accepted

Deadline: Pre-work must be received 5 business days before your scheduled Diagnostic call. Failure to submit pre-work by this deadline results in automatic session cancellation with no refund.

Phase 1: Live Diagnostic Session

  • Duration: 60-90 minutes
  • Format: Phone call (not video)
  • Your Role: Answer questions, clarify context, map decision-making structures
  • Our Role: Identify structural fractures (not symptoms), map leverage points

You will receive a verbal debrief during the call and a confirmation email within 24 hours.

Phase 2: Written Analysis Delivery

Within 3-4 business days of your Diagnostic call, you will receive your Crisis Infrastructure Brief via email (PDF format).

The Brief includes:

  • Crisis map (visual representation of fractures)
  • 3-5 structural problems identified
  • 3-5 recommended interventions (ranked by complexity/impact)
  • Actionable path forward (what you can do immediately without Sprint work)
  • Contingency plans
  • Sprint preview (if applicable)
