This often looks like organizations with 100 to 2,000 employees, growing teams, cross-functional work, stretched leadership, and operational patterns that keep repeating without a clear owner.

Work is happening. People are trying. Meetings are full. Priorities are active.

But something is still slowing execution.

The Operations Audit is for leaders who can feel the friction, but need a clearer view of what's creating it.

How the Audit Works

1

Feasibility Gate

Before scheduling an audit, we confirm three things:

  • The business problem is real enough to matter.
  • The evidence needed for the audit is available.
  • Leadership has enough capacity or authority to act on what the audit reveals.

If the engagement doesn't pass those gates, we pause, narrow the scope, or recommend a better starting point.

2

Intake and Evidence Review

We gather context around the operating issue, the people involved, the workflows affected, and the decisions leadership needs to make.

3

Diagnostic Analysis

We examine the evidence for structural patterns, friction points, visibility gaps, and operational risk signals.

4

A Clear Written Picture

You receive a clear summary of what's slowing execution, what risks are most important, and what decisions should come next.

5

Decision Support

The audit closes with a practical action path so leadership can decide what to address first, what to monitor, and what to defer.
